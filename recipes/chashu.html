Chashu

What is chashu?

Chashu is a popular topping for ramen. It's made by braising a rolled up marinated pork belly for two hours.

Ingredients (for ten)

  • 1 pork belly
  • 900ml soy sauce
  • 200ml dry sake
  • 200ml mirin
  • 1 bulb garlic
  • 4 slices ginger
  • 2 spring onions

Steps

  1. Fold the pork belly in half and tie into a round shape.
  2. Mix the soy sauce, dry sake, mirin, garlic (cut in half), ginger and spring onion in a pot and add the pork belly. Make sure it's submerged.
  3. Simmer for two hours, making sure the pork stays submerged. Add more sake/water when necessary.
  4. Remove the pork belly from the pot and cool in the fridge, preferably overnight.
  5. Remove the ties and slice thinly.
