Tonkotsu Broth

What is tonkotsu broth?

Tonkotsu broth is a soup base used when making ramen that's made by boiling pork bones for hours to achieve a thick, milky texture. It's typically flavoured with shoyu (soy sauce) to add a salty and umami taste.

Ingredients (for four)

  • 1lb pork feet
  • 1lb pork neck
  • 6 cups room temperature water

Steps

  1. Fill a pot half-full with water and bring to the boil.
  2. Add the pork feet and neck. Scoop out whatever rises to the top until you don't see any more scum - this will take 20-40 minutes.
  3. Strain the pork bones and clean the pot. Thoroughly wash each bone and add them back to the pot with the water from the ingredients list.
  4. Boil for 6-10 hours; the longer you boil, the more viscous the broth will be. Keep adding water as the amount in the pot decreases. Stir every 15-25 minutes.
  5. Strain the contents of the pot to leave you with just a liquid broth.
